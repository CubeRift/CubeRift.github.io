Plain English summary: We store your username (hashed password), and any code you upload. We don't sell your data. We use Google AdSense on the public page. You can ask us to delete everything at any time.
Section 01

Information We Collect

We collect only what is necessary to provide the CodeVault service:

  • Account information: Your chosen username and a SHA-256 hash of your password. We never store your plain-text password.
  • Code content: The lab programs and snippets you upload to your private vault, stored as private GitHub Gists.
  • Usage data: Basic page view information collected automatically by our hosting provider (Netlify/GitHub Pages) and Google Analytics, including approximate location, browser type, and pages visited.
  • Contact information: If you contact us via the Contact page, the name and email address you provide.

We do not collect phone numbers, government IDs, payment information, or any sensitive personal data.

Section 02

How We Use Your Information

  • To authenticate you when you log in to your vault
  • To store and retrieve your uploaded code snippets
  • To display public snippets on the browse page
  • To respond to support and contact requests
  • To analyse site traffic and improve the service
  • To serve relevant advertisements on the public library page

We do not use your information for automated profiling, sell it to data brokers, or share it with any third party for marketing purposes.

Section 03

Data Storage & Security

Your code is stored as private GitHub Gists on GitHub's infrastructure, which is subject to GitHub's own privacy and security practices. Your account credentials are stored in a private GitHub Gist containing only your username and hashed password.

Passwords are hashed using the SHA-256 algorithm in your browser before being transmitted. This means no plaintext password ever leaves your device.

While we take reasonable precautions, no internet service is 100% secure. We recommend using a unique password for CodeVault and not storing highly sensitive or confidential code in any cloud service.

Section 04

Cookies & Tracking

CodeVault uses sessionStorage (not persistent cookies) to keep you logged in during your browser session. This data is automatically cleared when you close your browser tab.

The public library page may use cookies set by:

  • Google AdSense — for ad personalisation and frequency capping
  • Google Analytics — for anonymous visitor statistics

You can opt out of personalised advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings. You can also use your browser's built-in cookie controls to block or delete cookies.

Section 05

Third-Party Services

CodeVault relies on the following third-party services to operate:

  • GitHub (Microsoft): Code storage via GitHub Gists API. Subject to GitHub's Privacy Statement.
  • Cloudflare Workers: Our API proxy layer. Subject to Cloudflare's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts: Font delivery. Google may log requests. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google AdSense (public page only): Advertisement serving. See Section 6 below.

We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third-party services.

Section 06

Advertising

The public library page (public.html) displays advertisements served by Google AdSense to help cover hosting and development costs. Your private vault (index.html) contains no advertisements.

Google AdSense may use cookies and device identifiers to show personalised ads based on your browsing history. You can control ad personalisation at google.com/settings/ads.

We comply with the Google AdSense programme policies and do not place ads near sensitive content or in a manner designed to deceive users.

Section 07

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Correction: Ask us to correct inaccurate information
  • Deletion: Request complete removal of your account and all uploaded code
  • Portability: Ask for your uploaded code in a downloadable format
  • Withdrawal: Stop using the service at any time — your data will remain until you request deletion

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@codevault.app. We will respond within 30 days.

Section 08

Children's Privacy

CodeVault is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has created an account, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

Section 09

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of CodeVault after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

For significant changes, we will display a notice on the site homepage.
